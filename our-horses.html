Our Horses

Horses are magnificent creatures that have played an integral role in human history and culture for thousands of years. These remarkable animals have been our companions in agriculture.

Friesian

  • Age: 4
  • Gender: Male

The Friesian horse, often simply referred to as the Friesian, is a distinctive and elegant breed known for its striking appearance, gentle temperament, and historical significance. The Friesian horse hails from the Friesland region of the Netherlands, dating back to the 16th century or earlier. It is one of the oldest horse breeds in Europe and has a rich history.

Temperament
  • Friesians are known for their gentle and friendly temperament. They are often described as people-oriented and willing to please.
  • They have a calm and steady disposition, which makes them suitable for various equestrian disciplines, including dressage and driving.

Andalusian

  • Age: 2
  • Gender: Female

The Andalusian, also known as the Pure Spanish Horse or Pura Raza Española (PRE), is a distinguished and ancient horse breed with a rich history and notable characteristics. Andalusians are descendants of horses brought to the Iberian Peninsula by various ancient civilizations, including the Carthaginians and Romans.

Temperament
  • Friesians are known for their gentle and friendly temperament. They are often described as people-oriented and willing to please.
  • They have a calm and steady disposition, which makes them suitable for various equestrian disciplines, including dressage and driving.

Arabian

  • Age: 8
  • Gender: Male

The Arabian horse is one of the world's oldest and most distinguished horse breeds, known for its distinctive appearance, intelligence, stamina, and historical significance. Bedouin tribes of the Arabian Peninsula selectively bred these horses for traits like endurance, agility, and loyalty, which allowed them to thrive in the harsh desert conditions.

Temperament
  • Friesians are known for their gentle and friendly temperament. They are often described as people-oriented and willing to please.
  • They have a calm and steady disposition, which makes them suitable for various equestrian disciplines, including dressage and driving.

Percheron

  • Age: 1
  • Gender: Male

The Percheron is a powerful and popular breed of draft horse known for its strength, versatility, and gentle temperament. The Percheron breed originated in the Perche region of western France, hence its name. It is one of the oldest and most well-known draft horse breeds in the world.

Temperament
  • Percheron are known for their gentle and docile nature. They are generally easygoing and cooperative, which makes them suitable for various work and equestrian tasks.
  • Their calm temperament makes them suitable for working in various environments, from farms to urban settings.

Mustang

  • Age: 3
  • Gender: Female

Mustangs are feral horses that roam freely in the western United States. They are known for their wild and untamed nature, as well as their historical significance.

Temperament
  • Many Mustangs are naturally curious animals. They are known to investigate their surroundings and can be quick learners when exposed to new environments and experiences.
  • Mustangs are generally considered intelligent and can learn quickly when provided with consistent and patient training. They have a strong sense of self-preservation, which can make them alert and responsive.
